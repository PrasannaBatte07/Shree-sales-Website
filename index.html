Safty Info

1. Read the manual: Always read the manual that comes with your power tool before using it. Familiarize yourself with the tool's features, safety precautions, and operating instructions.

2. Wear protective gear: When using power tools, it's important to wear proper protective gear, such as safety glasses, earplugs, gloves, and a dust mask. This can help protect you from flying debris, loud noises, and harmful dust and fumes.

3. Use the right tool for the job: Make sure you're using the right tool for the task at hand. Using the wrong tool or accessory can increase the risk of injury or damage to the tool.

4. Inspect the tool: Before using a power tool, inspect it for any damage or defects. Check the cord, plug, and switches to make sure they're in good condition.

5. Use in a safe location: Use power tools in a clean, dry, and well-lit area. Avoid using them in wet or damp conditions, and keep them away from flammable materials.

6. Secure the workpiece: Make sure the workpiece is properly secured before using a power tool. This can help prevent it from slipping or moving during use, which can be dangerous.

7. Be aware of your surroundings: Always be aware of your surroundings when using power tools. Keep other people and pets away from the work area, and make sure you have a clear line of sight.

8. Turn off and unplug when not in use: When you're finished using a power tool, turn it off and unplug it. Store it in a safe place where it won't be damaged or cause harm to anyone.
